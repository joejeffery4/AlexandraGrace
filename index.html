The sessions

Two ways to get strong

Classical Pilates and high-intensity reformer work share a love of control and time under tension — but they feel different in the body. Not sure which suits you? We'll work it out together.

01 — Pilates

Reformer & mat Pilates

Precise, controlled movement that builds deep core strength, mobility and posture. Ideal for rehab, building a foundation, or training smarter alongside other sport.

  • Core stability, alignment and control
  • Gentle on joints, scalable to any level
  • Great for injury recovery and posture
02 — Intensity

High-intensity reformer training

High-intensity, low-impact strength work on slow, deliberate tempos. Expect to shake. Brilliant for muscular endurance, tone and torching a workout in 45 focused minutes.

  • Slow, intense, low-impact resistance
  • Builds endurance and lean strength fast
  • Efficient — serious results in less time

About

Hi, I'm Alexandra

I trained at The Royal Ballet School, and that early grounding in precision, discipline and how the body truly moves still shapes every session I teach. Ballet taught me that real strength is built slowly, with control — and that's the principle at the heart of my coaching.

Over the past 10+ years I've taught reformer and mat Pilates to private clients and at some of London's most respected studios — including Heartcore, MAD, Reformer Room, Frame and BXR. I'm Reformer certified, and I tailor everything to you: your body, your level and your goals. No two clients are the same, so no two sessions are either.
